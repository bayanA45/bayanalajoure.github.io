What it is

Albayraq is a pioneering student-led research project to design and manufacture Jordan's first fully composite surveillance UAV. The project united mechanical, electrical, and AI expertise over two years — from research and theory through design, testing, and prototyping — culminating in a working 3.2m fixed-wing UAV.

My role — Electrical Systems Lead

PCB Design: Designed a custom multi-rail Power Distribution Board (PDB) from schematic through fabrication. Phase 1: 4–6S battery input, four regulated rails (3.3V, 5V, 7.4V, 12V), 24A max continuous, with safety and protection circuits. Phase 2 Smart PDB: added ultrasonic switching to suppress audible noise and EMI; high-power loads (Jetson Nano) powered directly from battery to ensure electrical isolation.

Curing oven: Built the complete electrical system for an industrial composite-curing oven reaching 100°C — 220V wiring, relay control, BMO180 temperature sensor, indicator lighting, full prototype board assembly.

Systems integration: Wiring harness design, component procurement, cross-department coordination, full financial tracking and documentation for the project.

Concept

Inspired by MIT's M-Blocks and Big Hero 6, Bunyan is a modular robotics system consisting of a builder robot and passive voxel cells. The builder autonomously picks up voxels and places them with precision to construct structures — with the long-term vision of robots assembling other robots. Phase 1 focused on the foundational engineering; Phase 2 will extend to full assembly.

My contributions

Mathematical model: Developed a fully verified kinematic and dynamic model for one robot limb — tested and validated in Autodesk Inventor.

Digital twin: Built from scratch a live digital twin integrating MATLAB App Designer and Autodesk Inventor. Users select a predefined part and control its parameters via MATLAB sliders — changes reflect in real time inside Inventor.

Mechanical design: Co-designed the limb structure and a novel magnetic joint system with no prior design precedent.

Prototyping: Fabricated components using SLA resin, PETG, and PLA 3D printing.

CNC Router — 4th Axis Engineering

The OMNI CNC router was a 3-axis machine. Added a 4th axis driver and relay switch system that toggles between the Y and U axes — giving the machine 4-axis capability without adding a 4th motor. Rewired all on/off switches, added an emergency stop button, and rebuilt the full electrical connection. The modification works and is in active use.

3D Printer Troubleshooting

Diagnosed and repaired 3 non-functional lab printers (Prusa, Ultimaker, Ender series) through systematic troubleshooting. All returned to service for 80+ daily users. Also attempted a 4th-axis motor and driver addition on the laser cutter.
